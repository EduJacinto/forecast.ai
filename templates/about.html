How it works

Built on LSTM

A deep-dive into the architecture behind the predictions.

01

Data collection

We pull 2 years of daily closing prices from Yahoo Finance via yfinance. Using 2 years gives the model enough medium-term patterns to generalise well without being dragged down by stale regimes.

02

Preprocessing

Prices are scaled to [0, 1] with MinMaxScaler. We then build sliding windows of 60 trading days — each window becomes one training sample, and the target is the very next day's scaled close.

03

LSTM architecture

Two stacked LSTM layers (64 units each) with 20% dropout to prevent overfitting, followed by a 32-unit dense ReLU layer and a single linear output neuron. Compiled with Adam and MSE loss.

04

Training split

80% of sequences are used for training, 20% reserved as a validation set. Training runs for 20 epochs — enough to converge without heavy compute cost. Validation loss is monitored throughout.

05

Forecasting

We feed the last 60-day window into the trained model, append each predicted value back into the window, and roll forward — producing 30 business days of predictions in a single autoregressive pass.
